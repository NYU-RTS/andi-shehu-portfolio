DataBarOnTheRun

A small NYC series built around conversation, hospitality, and people working across data and business.

DataBarOnTheRun was a small NYC event series I organized to bring together people from data, analytics, AI, startups, and business in a setting that was more conversational than a typical meetup.

Some gatherings leaned more social. Others were closer to roundtables. The point was to keep the room small enough for people to actually talk, meet one another, and leave with useful connections.

New York City Small gatherings Hosted format Data and AI network

What it was

Small room, real conversation

I wanted something closer to a good room than a formal event. That meant fewer people, more conversation, and enough structure to help the evening move without turning it into a talk or a panel.

The work included organizing the format, bringing people together, hosting the room, and making sure the atmosphere felt open and easy. The best part of it was seeing technical people, founders, and business-minded attendees actually spend time in conversation with one another.

  • Brought together people from data, AI, startups, and business in NYC.
  • Kept the format small enough for real discussion instead of passive attendance.
  • Handled the hosting, pacing, and feel of the room.
